Step 1: Know Your Income (The Simple Way)

Write down ALL the money you expect to bring in this month. Don't forget irregular income from side jobs or benefits.

Step 2: Track Your Needs (The Must-Pay Bills)

  • Rent/Mortgage (Housing)
  • Food (Groceries, not takeout)
  • Utilities (Electric, Gas, Water)
  • Transportation (Bus pass, gas)
  • Minimum Debt Payments

Step 3: The 50/30/20 Rule (Simplified for Real Life)

A simple guide to help you manage your paycheck:

  • **50%:** Needs (Housing, Food, Bills)
  • **30%:** Wants (Fun money, takeout, non-essential clothing)
  • **20%:** Savings & Debt Payoff (Your future!)

The Danger Zone: Predatory Loans

**AVOID** payday loans and car title loans. They have hidden fees and interest rates that can trap your family in debt.

Two Simple Ways to Pay Down Debt:

  1. **Debt Snowball:** Pay off the smallest debt first. It gives you quick wins and keeps you motivated!
  2. **Debt Avalanche:** Pay off the debt with the highest interest rate first. This saves you the most money in the long run.
